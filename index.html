About Tutorial

Explanation of Tutorial.

Step 1

Firstly you want a rough sketch of what you'll be drawing, be bold with your lines and try not to draw too light, it is all digitilazed afterall and the opacity of the sketch can be lowered to make linearting easier.

Step 2

Next you'll want to do a basic outline lineart! It should mostly be black though you can make it any colour you find best fitting for your art style.

Step 3

At this point we should be filling in the artwrok with flat colour. This means to use only a single colour per area of the characters without any highlighting or shading.

Step 4

Now is when we should begin shading. Use a darker colour of each flat colour to begin finding the appropriate places to shade. It's important to remember where the light is coming from in this step. A handy tip is to move the colour slider a little when choosing a darker colour to use.

Step 5

Now we'll begin texturing and adding highlights. Sometimes texturing can vary from character to character but I personally enjoy adding a blueish/purple hue into the base shading regardless of the colour. Highlighting in a light yellow/cream works well for warmer colours, but for colder colours I'd recommend a light purple or light blue.

Step 6

Backgrounds can be tricky but that's the next step. This is a specific case given this artwork in particular is high in the sky, so the ground below only needs to be suggested with some green. Rivers may vauguely be seen, it's good to have a noticable horizon with this sort of angle.

Step 7

Here we are adding a few clouds, starting with darker colours and working to a light colour on top, there is also a noticable overlay across the entire background giving it a lighter hue. Overlays like this are often done with a light yellow or blue.

Step 8

This step uses another overlay across the background. Using a darker pink on the overlay setting can give everything a very pink feel, giving the impression better that this artwork is set during sunset.

Final step

For the final step we do touch up work. Maybe adding a border around the sides of the artwork if wanted, or adding some soft airbrashes of clouds overtop of the characters to give the piece more depth. There's also the option to add small final details like the tears shown on the characters faces. And of course to top it all off a signature from yourself will official give the piece your ownership!
